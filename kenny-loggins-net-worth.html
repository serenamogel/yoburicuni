Kenny Loggings Early And Personal Life

Kenny Loggins, born on January 7, 1948, in Everett, Washington, is the youngest of three brothers.

His mother, Lina, was a homemaker, and his father, Robert George Loggins, worked as a salesman.

The family initially lived in Detroit before settling in Alhambra, California.

After graduating from San Gabriel Mission High School in 1966, Kenny Loggins pursued his passion for music, forming his first band called The Second Helping.

They released three singles between 1968 and 1969 under Viva Records, known for their “punky folk-pop” style.

Before joining the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band, Loggins briefly played guitar for the New Improved Electric Prunes.

Kenny was married to Eva Ein from 1978 to 1990, and they had three children, including Crosby Loggins, a musician who won the MTV reality show “Rock the Cradle.”

In 1992, Kenny married Julia Cooper, a former colon therapist, and they had two children together before divorcing in 2004.

Kenny admitted to being blindsided by Julia’s decision to leave, and the divorce had a significant financial impact on him, exacerbated by the global financial crisis that followed.

Kenny Loggins Career

In the 1970s, Kenny Loggins delved deep into his musical journey, primarily as part of the duo Loggins and Messina, alongside fellow musician Jim Messina.

During this period, Loggins was known for his ballads, and many of his compositions were recorded by the duo.

He even secured a six-album contract with Columbia Records, largely influenced by Messina.

Under this partnership, Loggins released his debut album, produced by Messina, and was joined by the Kenny Loggins Band.

As time went on, Loggins and Messina started functioning more like two separate solo artists sharing the same record rather than collaborating creatively.

This shift was due to a growing sense of competition between them.

After leaving Loggins and Messina, Kenny Loggins had a fruitful solo career with hit albums like “Celebrate Me Home” and “Nightwatch” (including “Whenever I Call You Friend”).

Although his later solo albums in the ’80s were not as commercially successful and were released less frequently, he continued to cater to the adult contemporary audience with efforts like “Leap of Faith” (1991), “The Unimaginable Life” (1997), and “December” (1998).

In 1994, he ventured into children’s music with the release of “Return to Pooh Corner” and followed it up with “More Songs from Pooh Corner” in 2000.

In 2005, Loggins and Messina reunited for a triumphant tour, album, and concert video.

After two years, Loggins released “How About Now,” marking his first solo album in four years, followed by “All Join In” in 2009.

With a career spanning three decades, Loggins still occasionally performs and records music.

He’s also a member of Blue Sky Riders, a country music trio that includes Gary Burr and Georgia Middleman.

The group released their debut album, “Finally Home,” in January 2013.

As for Kenny Loggins’ net worth, it has been shaped by his illustrious career in music, spanning both group and solo endeavours.

Kenny Loggins Career Awards And Nominations

At the height of his career, Loggins was dubbed the soundtrack king.

His musical journey began with Jim Messina at ABC-Dunhill, where they collaborated on numerous songs.

Messina produced Loggins’ six-track album with Columbia, a hit among college students, leading to the formation of their duo band.

Together, they released five albums, selling 16 million records.

In 1977, Loggins launched his solo career, releasing albums like “Keep the Fire” (1979) and “High Adventure” (1982).

He focused on guitar and wrote songs for Michael McDonald, including the Grammy-winning:

“What a Fool Believes” (1980) and “This Is It” (1979).

Loggins also crafted memorable soundtracks, including “I’m Alright,” “Meet Me Half Way,” and “Playing with the Boys.”

Kenny Loggins Philanthropic Work

Kenny Loggins Investments

Loggins has had real estate as one of his predominant area of investment.
