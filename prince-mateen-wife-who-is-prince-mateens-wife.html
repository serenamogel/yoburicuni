Prince Mateen wife-The Prince of Brunei, Abdul Mateen ibni Hassanal Bolkiah was born on AUGUST 10, 1991, in Raja Isteri Pengiran Anak Saleha Hospital, Bandar Seri Begawan, Brunei.

Who is Prince Mateen’s wife?

Prince Mateen is not married at the time of filing this report. However, he is expected to tie the knot with Anisha Rosnah binti Adam, the granddaughter of Pehin Dato Haji Isa, a special advisor to the Sultan of Brunei in January 2024.

Their marriage ceremony has been declared by Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah and the celebration will last for ten days and included a number of religious ceremonies.

Prince Mateen career

At the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst, Prince Mateen and 200 candidates started their Commissioning Course for Regular Army Officers on May 9, 2010.

Prince Mateen was promoted to the rank of Second Lieutenant on April 15, 2011, after completing the commissioning course at the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst in the United Kingdom.

The Borneo Bulletin reported that Mateen was later elevated to the rank of Substantive Lieutenant on July 9, 2012. On August 31, 2016, he received an Acting Captain promotion, which would take effect on May 9 of the following year.

On April 28, 2017, he finished his seven-month Elementary Flying Training at RAF Cranwell. Later, in March 2018, Prince Mateen graduated from the Defence Helicopter Flying School at RAF Shawbury and received his flying brevet (wings), which his father presented to him.

His father provided his approval for him to be given honorary membership in the Special Forces Regiment on June 30, 2019, as part of the festivities marking the 58th anniversary of the Royal Brunei Armed Forces (RBAF).

Prince Mateen was elevated to the position of Major on May 20, 2021. Prince Mateen had completed the All Arms Commando Course (AACC) later that year, which lasted for eleven weeks from the 20th of September to the 2nd of December at the Commando Training Centre Royal Marines (CTCRM) in the United Kingdom and earned him the Green Beret.

He had spent the preceding two weeks training with 64 other brand-new parachutists, according to the RAF. The award ceremony was conducted at RAF Brize Norton on September 23, 2022, after he successfully finished Basic Parachute Training.

Later on October 19, he and his father went to Brussels for the 12th Asia-Europe Meeting (ASEM), where Brunei asked that negotiations for a Free Trade Agreement (FTA) between the European Union (EU) and ASEAN be restarted.

The rituals, which concluded a transition that began with his father’s abdication earlier that year, were attended by him and his father.
