About

Prince and The Revolution was a popular American rock band that was active from 1979 to 1986. The band was led by the legendary musician, singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, and producer Prince, who was known for his eclectic music style and flamboyant stage presence.

The band released several critically acclaimed albums during their career, including “Purple Rain”, “1999”, and “Sign o’ the Times”. Their music was characterized by a unique blend of rock, funk, soul, R&B, and pop influences, and they were widely regarded as one of the most innovative and influential bands of their time.

In addition to Prince, the original lineup of The Revolution included Wendy Melvoin (guitar), Lisa Coleman (keyboards), Brownmark (bass), Bobby Z (drums), and Doctor Fink (keyboards). The band collaborated closely with Prince on many of his most famous hits and played a key role in shaping his sound and image.
